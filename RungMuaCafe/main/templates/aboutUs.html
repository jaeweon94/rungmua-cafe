Our stories

Where our idea came from


"Rung Mua" means "Rainforest" in Vietnamese.

At first, when first thinking about starting a coffee shop with special theme, the coffee shop owners - Jaeweon and Minh - were having a headache pondering which theme to choose from, in order to make our coffee shop stand out creatively, as welll as give customers the best experience. I suggested that we should go with a manga theme or a game theme, considering these are a trend in Asian countries. But then Jaeweon asked me which drinks are the most favored in Vietnam, I suddenly thought of "chè"-the most popular sweet dessert among Vietnamese high-schoolers and students. It is not a bad idea, since "Chè" is so underrated for outsiders, while I, as a "Chè" crazy fan, was very let down not being able to find a whole "chè"-specific store abroad, no matter where I went (the US, France or Korea). If boba tea can become a new worldwide tendency, why not "chè"?

Since this shop will be selling Vietnamese traditional cuisine, it should have a Viet Nam themed with a Vietnamese name. So in the end, "Rung Mua" with a green and classic theme came up.

Our Coffee

Selected from Viet Nam's best source.

Coffee was introduced into Vietnam by the French in 1857. Now Viet Nam has become the second biggest coffee exporting country in the world.

97% of Vietnamese coffee comes from Robusta species. It is known for its low acidity and strong bitterness, thus is frequently used as instant coffee or the base for coffee mixture. Compared to Arabica, Robusta is easier to take care of, prevent diseases and have bigger crop productivity. It contains twice as much caffeine as the other coffee and more anti-oxydants. Overall, Viet Nam contributes 40% of the world's Robusta's quantity.

What makes Viet Nam's coffee special is that, besides having great productivity, coffee has been incorporated into Viet Nam's culture, as it appears in every Saigonese's breakfast, but also because it is consumed with Vietnamese "out-of-this-world" styles. Who would have thought that coffee could taste so good in mixture with yolk? And "cafe phin" filtered on spot will for sure leave instant coffee drinkers jawdrop.
